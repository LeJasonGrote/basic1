My Crap

My last posts depend on each other, and should be read in sequence. Articles not in bold are optional.

  • Assignment Statement Considered Harmful. Pervasive mutable state make programs more difficult to deal with. While this old news, most programmers don't know or don't care. It's a pitty, because simple remedies exist.
  • Defining Syntactic Sugar. I sometimes hear arguments about what is or is not syntactic sugar. This is an attempt at defining it.
  • Class-based Programming as Syntactic Sugar. Most of the time, "Objects Oriented" actually mean classes. Classes are nothing more than syntactic sugar, at least in statically typed languages such as Java and C++. Sugar is not useless, but seeing past it helps us understand classes better.
  • Taboo "OO". The term "OO" is so overleaded that we should stop using it. Better substitutes are "classes and "prorotypes".
  • How Class based Programming Sucks.. Classes are vastly sub-optimal. Functionnal programing is far better
